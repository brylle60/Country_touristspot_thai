Thai Cuisine

Experience the vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and perfect balance of sweet, spicy, sour, and salty that make Thai food world-famous.

A Culinary Journey

Thai cuisine is celebrated globally for its complex flavors and fresh ingredients. Each region offers distinctive dishes, from the coconut-based curries of the south to the herb-rich, spicy dishes of the northeast.

Street food is an essential part of Thai culinary culture, with some of the most authentic and delicious dishes found at roadside stalls and markets. Meanwhile, fine dining establishments are elevating traditional recipes with modern techniques and presentation.

Must-Try Dishes

Pad Thai

This stir-fried noodle dish combines sweet, sour, and savory flavors with peanuts and fresh lime.

Tom Yum Goong

Hot and sour shrimp soup with lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and chili.

Green Curry

Aromatic coconut-based curry with fresh green chilies, Thai basil, and your choice of protein.

Top Dining Destinations

Where to Eat in Thailand

Bangkok: From Michelin-starred Gaggan and Jay Fai's famous crab omelette to vibrant street food scenes at Chinatown and Chatuchak Market.

Chiang Mai: Try khao soi (curry noodle soup) at local favorites like Khao Soi Lam Duan or explore the Sunday Night Market for endless culinary treasures.

Phuket: Enjoy fresh seafood at Kan Eang@Pier or authentic southern Thai cuisine at Raya Restaurant housed in a historic Sino-Portuguese building.
